Olive- A Health and Wellness App

An Overview

Olive is a mobile application designed to promote user well-being by offering a wide range of health and wellness resources. The goal of the project was to provide a users a platform that empowers them to take control of their health journey. This involved creating an intuitive, accessible, as well as an aesthetically pleasing app.

This project was conceived and developed as a part of a CareerFoundry UX Bootcamp, in which I undertook various roles and responsibilities, encompassing every aspect of the project's lifecycle. I assumed the role of UX researcher to understand user pain points, needs, and preferences. As a UX designer, I utilized an iterative design approach to continually refine the app's prototype based on user feedback and user testing. This holistic approach allowed for a comprehensive understanding of the project from its inception to completion.

Problem Statement

Olive users need an intuitive app with a personalized user experience to track and gain insights into their health metrics, ultimately allowing them to effectively monitor their comprehensive health journey to improve their health and wellness. We will know this to be true when we see an increase in the number of users who customize their profile, adjust settings and use personalized options within the app.

Solution

Provide a personalized user experience on Olive for individuals to track and improve their health and wellness.

Features which must be included in the app are:
  • profile page where users can adjust their needs such as dietary preferences and notifications
  • ability to access health resources
  • nutrition logging and health journaling
  • health management by letting users track and record their doctor visits, (as well as medications, vaccines, and treatments)
  • tracking fitness via a wearable device

As users input their health vitals on the app, there should a clear way of accessing their progress and reviewing their journey. This can be depicted via descriptive progress charts and pie charts.

I began creating my low to mid fidelity wireframes. At this stage, the wireframes were defined enough for some user testing. Based on 2 tests, I've made a few alternations and moved on to creating high-fidelity prototypes.

We made it this far! Now time for user testing...

I conducted a moderated usability test with 6 participants for Olive, focusing on the overall user experience and specific features. The app aims to help users track their health, access educational health related resources, and engage with the community. The test will evaluate the usability and effectiveness of the app in meeting user needs.

Goals:
  • Assess the app's dashboard and ease of use
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of the onboarding process
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of tracking health data and accessing resources
  • Gather feedback on the overall user experiences and satisfaction with the appâ€™s features
Test Objectives:
  • Evaluate the onboarding process
  • Evaluate the clarity and intuitiveness of the app's dashboard/ homepage
  • Assess the ease of tracking health data and accessing related features (appointments, medications, etc.)
  • Access the health education resources section
  • Logging nutritional information with ease
  • Identify any usability issues or areas for improvements in the user flow and interactions

UI Designing to bring the ideas to life

Once the usability issues were resolved, I moved on to design the final screens in Figma. The design follows a vibrant and engaging approach, featuring lively and colorful illustrations. Illustrations add a touch of playfulness, thus making it more approachable.

Rooted in Material Design principles, the interface maintains simplicity and cleanliness throughout as the participants in user testing had desired. While learning about design, I understood the importance of inclusivity. My design includes accessibility features such as voice navigation and dark mode. In this design I wanted to strike a harmonious balance of visual appeal and usability. I wanted to create an enjoyable and accessible app for users to track their health, access resources, and engage with the community.

Provinding a personalized experience was essential

Tracking ones health and wellness journey is a very personal experience, therefore I provided options of personalization in multiple ways. Keeping accessibility in mind, I allowed for dark mode, screen reading, and text size adjustments.

Users are able to gamify their journey and track their achievements and remain motivated. There are ways to adjust dietary preferences, manage notifications, and pair their wearable fitness devices. Another important thing is that as the user keeps interacting with the app, the app will keep recommending resources which may interest the users on their dashboard.
